## Where Elasticsearch is actually different

Elasticsearch gives you **more flexible scoring tools**, which makes some custom ranking easier to express in a single query:

- **`function_score` query** — boost results inline by field math, e.g. "score × log(followers + 1)"
- **`decay functions`** — built-in geo/date decay, e.g. "boost by closeness, with score halving every 50 km"
- **`script_score`** — write custom scoring formulas (Painless scripting language)
- **`rank_features` field type** — purpose-built for ranking signals like popularity scores

In Meilisearch, you usually do these things by **re-ranking in your Go code** after getting hits, because Meilisearch's scoring API is simpler.

Honestly — **probably not**. Meilisearch is the right choice for your app because:

| | Meilisearch | Elasticsearch |
|---|---|---|
| Memory needed | ~100 MB | 1-2 GB minimum |

Elasticsearch is overkill unless you have **tens of millions** of users/posts AND need complex aggregations. Even Instagram doesn't run Elasticsearch for user search — they use specialized in-house systems with re-ranking layers.
