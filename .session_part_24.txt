## The 6 upgrades

**1. Two-tower embeddings** (`embeddings.go`)
Every piece of content gets a "fingerprint" (a list of 32 numbers) based on its category, creator, mood, quality, freshness. **2. Seen-filter** (`seen_filter.go`)
Remembers every post shown to a user for the last 12 hours. Hard rule: never show the same thing twice. Before, we only filtered out content they *interacted* with — now we filter anything they *saw*, even if they scrolled past.

**4. Multi-source candidates** (`candidate_sources.go`)
Instead of pulling content from one pool, we now pull from 5 pools in parallel: fresh posts, trending, people you follow, people similar to you, and content matching your fingerprint. Then we blend them. If one source fails, the others still work.

**5. Position-bias correction + Platt calibration** (`learning_to_rank.go`, `calibration.go`)
The learning model used to unfairly believe "top-slot content is great" just because it got shown first. Now we mathematically discount that bias. Platt calibration converts raw model scores into actual probabilities, so a "0.8" really means "80% chance this user engages."

**6. Anti-loop detector + soft-mix bandit** (`anti_loop.go`, `bandit.go`)
Watches for 4 "stuck" patterns: 4 skips in a row, same category dominating, same creator flooding the feed, or "dopamine budget" collapsing. When any of these fire, the algorithm switches to a different strategy (discovery, calming, fresh creators) that it hasn't tried yet this session.
